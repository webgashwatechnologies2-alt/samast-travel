Why Visit Uttarakhand?

Uttarakhand is part of the Northern India Himalayan Region and is famously known as the Land of the Gods (Devbhumi). The state capital is Dehradun, nestled in the Doon Valley at approximately 2,100 feet (640 meters) above sea level, while its highland districts like Badrinath and Kedarnath sit above 10,000 feet.

Known for its sacred pilgrimage sites, dense forests, and majestic Himalayan peaks, Uttarakhand draws pilgrims, trekkers, adventure seekers, and nature lovers from across the globe — offering experiences ranging from the Char Dham Yatra to thrilling river rafting on the Ganges.
